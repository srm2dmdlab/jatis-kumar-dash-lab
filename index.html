About the lab

The 2D Materials and Devices Laboratory is located at lab no. JC-314, JC Bose Block, SRM University-AP campus. We work on the growth and characterization of transition metal dichalcogenides and oxides — fabricating 2D layered materials such as NbS₂, SnS₂, and MoS₂ via physical vapor deposition and CVD, and pushing them toward real device applications.

Research Focus

Three threads, one material family

Large-area 2D growth

Sputtering and CVD synthesis of NbS₂, SnS₂, and MoS₂ films with tunable phase and bandgap, for optoelectronics and photovoltaics.

2D transition metal oxides

Van der Waals epitaxial growth of layered α-MoO₃ on hBN and graphene, probed by conductive AFM for tunneling-barrier applications.

Heterostructures & thermoelectrics

Building clean 2D TMDC heterostacks and exploring new chalcogenide/oxide materials for high-efficiency thermoelectric devices.
